# Product Vision

Status: Approved / Frozen — M0.0 (2026-07-21)

## Vision

Turn raw video and editorial intent into a transparent, editable, professional rough cut—without pretending that AI judgment is frame-perfect or replacing the human editor.

## Core problem

Editors and content creators often know what should remain in a video before they know the exact timecodes. They may already have:

- the original video;
- a transcript;
- selected phrases or a rewritten script;
- several recordings that might form a sequence;
- an idea such as “make this a concise, energetic two-minute story.”

Today they must manually find every phrase, set in/out points, build a timeline, and repeatedly audition alternatives.

## Product promise

The application converts textual selections and editorial intent into a **non-destructive timeline proposal** that remains inspectable and adjustable.

## Primary users

### 1. Transcript-driven creator

Has one long recording and already knows which passages should remain.

### 2. Video editor / producer

Has multiple takes, interviews, B-roll, or recordings and wants a strong first assembly before refining in Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve.

### 3. Content team

Wants consistent rough cuts from repeatable briefs while retaining human approval.

## Product principles

1. **Deterministic before generative.** Exact time and interval work belongs to code.
2. **AI proposes; humans approve.** Every AI-selected clip must be inspectable and replaceable.
3. **Non-destructive by default.** Source media is never modified.
4. **Local media first.** Original media stays local unless the user explicitly enables an upload-based workflow.
5. **Professional escape hatch.** The result must leave the app as a rendered file and as an editable timeline.
6. **Explain confidence, not certainty.** AI recommendations include reasons, alternatives, and confidence.
7. **Small milestones, frozen contracts.** Each milestone ends with automated evidence, smoke evidence, an independent judge, and a freeze.
